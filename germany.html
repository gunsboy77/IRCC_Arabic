Canadian Visit Visa Guide

For Syrian Permanent Residents in Germany with Children

Comprehensive guide to obtaining a Canadian Temporary Resident Visa (TRV)

Introduction

This guide provides step-by-step instructions for Syrian nationals with permanent residency in Germany who wish to apply for a Canadian visit visa (Temporary Resident Visa - TRV) for themselves and their three children. The process involves careful preparation of documents, online application, and demonstrating strong ties to Germany to ensure return after your visit.

Processing Time Note: Current processing times for visitor visas from Germany are approximately 30-45 days, but apply at least 3 months before your planned travel date.

Eligibility Requirements

1

Valid Travel Documents

All applicants must have valid passports. Syrian passports should be valid for at least 6 months beyond your planned stay in Canada.

2

German Residency Proof

You must provide proof of your permanent resident status in Germany (Aufenthaltserlaubnis or Niederlassungserlaubnis).

3

Financial Support

Demonstrate sufficient funds to cover your stay in Canada (minimum CAD $1,000 per month for main applicant plus CAD $500 per child).

4

Ties to Germany

Evidence showing you will return to Germany after your visit (employment, property, family ties, etc.).

5

No Criminal Record

You must not have a criminal record and may need to provide police certificates.

6

Medical Exam

Depending on your stay duration and activities, a medical examination may be required.

Required Documents Checklist

Important:

All documents not in English or French must be accompanied by a certified translation. Children under 18 require special documentation.

For All Applicants

  • Valid passport (for each person)
  • Two recent passport-sized photos
  • Proof of German permanent residency
  • Completed IMM 5257 application form
  • Family Information Form (IMM 5707)

Financial Documents

  • Bank statements (last 4 months)
  • Employment letter (with salary and leave approval)
  • Property deeds or rental agreements
  • Proof of investments or other assets

For Children

  • Birth certificates for all children
  • Consent letter from non-accompanying parent (if applicable)
  • School enrollment certificates
  • Custody documents (if applicable)

Travel Itinerary

  • Flight reservations (not purchased)
  • Accommodation details in Canada
  • Travel insurance proof
  • Purpose of visit explanation

Application Process

1. Create Online Account

Register on the Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) website. Choose "Apply to come to Canada" and create a GCKey account.

2. Complete Questionnaire

Answer eligibility questions to receive your personalized document checklist. Select "Visitor visa" as application type.

3. Fill Application Forms

Complete all required forms including IMM 5257 for each applicant and IMM 5707 for family information.

4. Upload Documents

Scan and upload all required documents according to your personalized checklist. Ensure files meet size and format requirements.

5. Pay Fees

Pay application fees online: CAD $100 per person. Biometrics fee: CAD $85 per person (if required). Total for family of four: CAD $740.

6. Submit Application

Review everything carefully and submit your application. You'll receive confirmation and application number immediately.

7. Biometrics Appointment

After submission, you'll receive a Biometrics Instruction Letter (BIL). Book appointment at VAC in Berlin, Düsseldorf, Frankfurt, Hamburg, or Munich.

8. Visa Decision

Processing typically takes 30-45 days. If approved, you'll receive a passport request letter. Submit passports to VAC for visa stamping.

Important Tips & Advice

Proving Ties to Germany

As a Syrian national, demonstrating strong ties to Germany is crucial. Include:

  • Employment contract showing permanent position
  • Property ownership or long-term lease agreement
  • Children's school enrollment certificates
  • Tax returns and social security contributions
  • Evidence of family members residing in Germany

Traveling with Children

Special requirements when applying for children:

  • Each child must have their own application
  • Notarized consent letter from non-traveling parent(s)
  • Birth certificates showing both parents' names
  • Custody documents if parents are separated/divorced

Application Strengthening Strategies

  • Write a detailed purpose of visit letter explaining your itinerary
  • Show return flight reservations (can be tentative)
  • Demonstrate financial capacity with multiple sources
  • Include previous travel history to show compliance with visas
  • Provide hotel bookings or invitation letter from Canadian host
